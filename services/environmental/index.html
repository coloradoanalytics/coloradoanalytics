Environmental Acoustics

An environmental acoustics analysis involves some combination of measurement, prediction, and modeling of noise that transmits through outdoor spaces. These efforts are often driven by regulatory or legal requirements, although it is not uncommon for noise producers to simply want to be good neighbors.

Some environmental acoustics studies consider only a single noise source and a single noise receiver. Others consider many noise sources affecting many receivers over areas and distances measured in miles. We are capable of measurement or analysis at any scale.
